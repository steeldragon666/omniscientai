Commercial model

Lease + subscription. Not per-seat, not per-token.

What you pay
  • 3-year appliance lease — Omniscient owns the hardware; you run it. Paid as incurred.
  • Sovereignty Subscription — signed updates, patching, the maintained AU compliance pack, and support. An ongoing service, not rent on intelligence.
  • Optional professional services — custom workflow builds beyond the standard estate.
What stops
  • Per-seat AI licences — unlimited seats and agents on the box.
  • Per-token metering — marginal cost of a query ≈ electricity.
  • Scattered SaaS subscriptions the estate replaces.

What's in the box

Not a chatbot. An operating estate.

AI

Local open-weight models sized to your team, with a full agentic skills library — skills, workflows and named agents — routed automatically to the right local model. For the hardest tasks, an opt-in, consented burst to frontier cloud models through the logged egress broker. Burst can be disabled outright.

Software estate

An open-source equivalent of nearly every business application — CRM, accounting, BI, documents, spreadsheets, comms — pre-integrated so the AI doesn't just chat, it acts. Proprietary APIs (bank feeds, government portals) connect through the same consented egress controls.

Memory

The second brain: every file, email and decision graphs into institutional memory split by department under your access boundaries — and it endures when people leave.

Governance

A policy broker gates every agent action — consequential actions need human or dual approval — and everything writes to a tamper-evident audit ledger. Full security architecture →

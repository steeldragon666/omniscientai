Security architecture

Sovereign by architecture.
Not by promise.

Two controls do most of the work: a single default-deny way out of the box, and a policy broker that gates every action an agent can take. Everything else exists to make those two controls provable. This page is the briefing your CISO will ask for — published, not gated.

Fig. 01 — control model · egress + action tiering + evidence

Control 01 · Network

The only path out is default-deny.

No inbound, ever

There is no inbound internet path to the appliance. Nothing on the internet can initiate a connection to the box — not us, not anyone.

One egress broker

All outbound traffic passes a single default-deny egress broker. Exactly two destinations can be allow-listed and pinned: consented burst (redacted, logged, zero-retention) and signed update pulls. All other egress is refused. Both paths can be disabled — one switch, or a physical air-gap, makes the box an island.

Segmented inside

App VLAN (web UI, SSO), AI VLAN (local models, vector DB, knowledge graph) and an MFA-gated management VLAN are separated internally, so a compromise in one plane doesn't grant the others.

Single-tenant by physics

Your models, your data, your graph — on hardware in your building, governed by Australian law. No co-tenants, no shared inference queues, no offshore processing by default.

Control 02 · Agent governance

Agents can't act unilaterally.

Agents never call tools directly. Every requested action passes a policy broker that assigns a risk class:

GREEN — runs immediately

Read & prepare: research, drafts, staging, analysis. No side effects outside the box.

BLUE — needs human or dual approval

Consequential: move money, touch personal data, send, publish, delete. Nothing in this class executes without sign-off.

RED — refused outright

Out of policy or unsafe — blocked regardless of what the model "decided". Injected instructions are tainted and cannot escalate.

Control 03 · Vendor access

You can lock us out. You can never be locked out.

No standing access

Support is customer-initiated, time-boxed, and recorded to your own ledger. There is no persistent vendor session, tunnel or account.

Keys stay with you

You hold the recovery keys. Omniscient's escrow share cannot decrypt alone — by design, not by policy.

Updates without a backdoor

The box pulls signed updates — no push, no open port — verifies signature + hash, and applies them as atomic, auto-rolling-back swaps in windows you approve.

Proof, not promises

Every control writes evidence.

All of it lands in a hash-chained, tamper-evident audit ledger: AI actions, approvals, admin actions, each burst and its consent record, every update and every support session. On demand, the box generates an auditor-ready evidence pack — data-residency attestation, access reports, model provenance, control status.

Essential Eight

Patching, application control, MFA, least privilege, backups and logging aligned out of the box.

Privacy Act 1988

Onshore data, no offshore disclosure by default, access/retention/deletion controls, automated-decision transparency — maintained as versioned, signed compliance updates.

Sector overlays

APRA CPS 234, health records handling, government classification and defence-adjacent hardening on the Regulated tier.

Telemetry: operational metrics only, opt-out, and never your content — proactive support without reading your business.

AI adoption and the R&DTI.
What actually qualifies.

You'll hear that AI adoption is "43.5% claimable." The truth is more useful: not all AI adoption is R&D — but genuine, well-documented experimentation with new AI-enabled ways of working can be. This page explains the test, the rates, and why the hardest part of any claim — contemporaneous evidence — is something the Omniscient appliance produces as a byproduct of the work.

Fig. 01 — eligibility, not entitlement · Division 355, ITAA 1997

The test

What Division 355 actually requires.

Core R&D activities are experimental activities whose outcome cannot be known or determined in advance, conducted through a systematic progression of work — hypothesis → experiment → observation → evaluation → logical conclusions — for the purpose of generating new knowledge. Supporting activities are those undertaken directly in relation to the core experiments.

Generally not R&D
  • Buying an off-the-shelf tool and using it as intended
  • Routine configuration, rollout and training
  • "Whole of project" claims where only a slice was experimental
  • Adopting a vendor's documented, proven pattern unchanged
Can be R&D — assessed case by case
  • Developing and testing new AI-enabled workflows or methods where the outcome is genuinely uncertain
  • Systematic experiments comparing approaches against measurable hypotheses
  • Novel integration of local models with your data, constraints and processes where existing knowledge doesn't answer the question
  • Only the experimental activities and their directly related supporting work — properly apportioned
The numbers

Rates, thresholds, deadlines.

Under $20M aggregated turnover

Refundable offset at the company tax rate + 18.5 percentage points — 43.5% for a 25%-rate company. Refundable means it can be paid in cash if you're in loss.

$20M and over

Non-refundable offset, tiered by R&D intensity (expenditure as a share of total expenses) — company rate + 8.5 points up to 2% intensity, + 16.5 points above it.

Registration

Activities must be registered with AusIndustry within 10 months of the end of the income year in which they occurred. Notional deductions generally need to exceed $20,000.

Scrutiny

Software-related claims attract regulator attention. The cases that fail are almost always evidence failures: no hypothesis recorded, no experiment log, no contemporaneous records. Rates and settings change — confirm current law with your adviser.
